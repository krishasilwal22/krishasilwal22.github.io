About Me

I’m an Economics and Data Science student with a strong interest in using data to analyze policies, economic trends, and social structures. My work involves statistical analysis, data visualization, and data-driven research to uncover insights that support informed decision-making.

This portfolio showcases projects where I have applied data analysis to explore fundamental concepts in programming, machine learning, and statistical modeling. Through these projects, I have gained experience in working with real-world datasets, refining my technical skills, and developing a structured approach to problem-solving. These experiences are helping me build a strong foundation in data analysis, which I aim to apply to research on economic inequality, labor markets, and gender-based economic disparities.

Hand Tracking & Object Interaction

What We Built

We developed a real-time hand tracking system that allows users to move blocks on a screen using only their hands.

Technologies Used:

  • Python – Program logic for hand tracking
  • OpenCV – Capturing video and detecting hand movements
  • cvzone – Simplified gesture-based interactions

Impact & Future Improvements

The system provided a touch-free way to interact with digital objects, making it an interesting step toward more accessible technology.
