Hand Tracking & Object Interaction

Developed a real-time hand tracking system using Python & OpenCV.

Expand ⬇

Problem Statement:

Traditional input devices like a mouse and keyboard are not accessible to everyone. Gesture-based systems offer an alternative, but many require precise hand movements, limiting accessibility.

What We Built:

We developed a real-time hand tracking system that detects hand movements and allows users to move digital blocks on the screen using just their hands.

Technologies Used:

  • Python – Logic for hand tracking
  • OpenCV – Capturing video and detecting hand positions
  • cvzone – Simplified gesture-based interactions

Impact & Shortcomings:

This system provided an accessible way to interact with digital objects without physical input devices, demonstrating how AI can improve accessibility. However, it still required some level of precision, which may be difficult for individuals with severe motor impairments.

Future Improvements:

Increase tracking precision, add customizable sensitivity levels, and expand the range of interactions beyond block movement.

Analyzing the Gender Pay Gap

Used Excel & regression models to explore wage disparities across states.

Expand ⬇

Problem Statement:

The gender pay gap persists across U.S. states, influenced by factors like minimum wage policies, labor force participation, and unemployment rates.

Technologies Used:

  • Excel – Data visualization & regression models

Findings & Shortcomings:

The regression analysis showed correlations between minimum wage and gender pay disparities, but data limitations prevented accounting for industry-specific differences.

Future Improvements:

Use more granular datasets with industry segmentation and integrate additional economic indicators for better predictive power.

Machine Learning for Employee Data

Built ML models to predict employee purchases using data cleaning & imputation.

Expand ⬇

Problem Statement:

Businesses often struggle with incomplete employee data, making it difficult to predict purchasing behavior and financial decisions.

What We Did:

We cleaned and formatted raw company data, handled missing values through imputation, and created machine learning models to predict employee purchase behavior based on job role, salary, and work location.

Technologies Used:

  • Excel – Data cleaning & visualization
  • Tableau – Confusion matrix

Impact & Future Improvements:

The model showed promising accuracy but lacked real-time adaptability. Future enhancements could involve deeper feature engineering and integration with live company data.
